Why Adults and Teens Choose Orthodontic Treatment

You might think orthodontics only matters for cosmetic reasons, but straight teeth do so much more than look good in photos. When your bite aligns properly, you reduce wear on your tooth enamel. You make brushing and flossing easier, which means fewer cavities down the road. Some patients even notice their jaw pain disappears once their teeth sit in the right position.

Dr. Dianne Pannes has worked with countless patients who waited years to pursue treatment, thinking they’d missed their window. Here’s the truth: your teeth can move at any age. Adults often make excellent orthodontic candidates because they’re motivated and consistent with their treatment plans.

SureSmile Clear Aligners: The Modern Approach

Traditional braces work wonderfully for many cases, but clear aligners have revolutionized how we approach orthodontics. SureSmile represents one of the most advanced systems available today. These custom-made aligners gradually shift your teeth into their ideal positions using precise 3D imaging and treatment planning.

What makes SureSmile different:

Dr. Brian May uses digital scans to map out your entire treatment journey before you even start. You’ll see a preview of your future smile, which feels real and exciting rather than daunting.

Your First Orthodontic Consultation

Walking into an orthodontic consultation can feel intimidating if you don’t know what to expect. Dr. James Safford makes this process straightforward and pressure-free. During your initial visit, we’ll take detailed images of your teeth and jaw and discuss what bothers you about your current smile and what you hope to achieve.

Some people need minor corrections that take just a few months. Others require more comprehensive treatment spanning a year or longer. We’ll explain exactly what your situation requires and give you realistic timelines. No surprises, no upselling treatments you don’t need.

Orthodontics for the Whole Family

One of the best parts about offering orthodontics in Newport at a family practice is that we can treat multiple generations under one roof. Dr. Aaron Ramos loves seeing siblings go through treatment together or parents and teenagers working toward their smile goals simultaneously.

Early orthodontic evaluations for children help us catch potential issues before they become complicated. Sometimes, we recommend a two-phase approach: We do minor interventions while kids still have baby teeth and complete treatment once their adult teeth come in. Other times, the best strategy involves waiting until their jaw growth stabilizes.

Life During Orthodontic Treatment

Your daily routine shifts slightly during treatment, but most patients adapt quickly. With SureSmile aligners, you wear them 20-22 hours per day, removing them only for meals and brushing. As your teeth gradually move, you switch to a new set of aligners every week or two.

Some mild soreness happens when you start a new aligner, but it typically fades within a day or two. This discomfort actually signals that your treatment works. Your teeth are shifting into better positions, even if you can’t see dramatic changes yet.

Regular checkups let us track your progress and make any necessary adjustments. These visits usually take less time than you’d spend at appointments with traditional braces.

The Investment in Your Smile

Cost concerns keep many people from pursuing orthodontic treatment, but financing options make care accessible for most budgets. We work with various payment plans and accept most dental insurance policies. Many insurance plans cover a portion of orthodontic treatment, even for adults.

Think of orthodontics as an investment that pays dividends for decades. Straighter teeth often mean fewer dental problems later, saving money over your lifetime. Plus, the confidence boost that comes with loving your smile? That’s genuinely priceless.
